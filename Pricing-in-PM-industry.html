At its core, this industry sells one thing: a shared system of record for how work gets done. Teams use these tools to plan tasks, communicate, track progress, and hit deadlines — instead of relying on email threads and spreadsheets.

Part 02

What Pricing Models Exist?

Two models dominate the PM software space. Each carries a different promise to the buyer — and a different business logic for the vendor.

Per-User Pricing

Most common

You pay a recurring fee for every seat on the team. Add a person, your bill goes up. Remove one, it goes down. The cost scales linearly with headcount.

Monthly Cost = Price/user × No. of users

Who uses it:

  • 💰 Asana — from $10.99/user/mo (Business)
  • 💰 monday.com — from $9/user/mo (Basic)
  • 💰 ClickUp — from $7/user/mo (Unlimited)
  • 💰 Jira — from $7.75/user/mo (Standard)
  • 💰 Notion — from $10/user/mo (Plus)

Implications for buyers:

  • ✅ Low entry cost for tiny teams (2–3 people)
  • ✅ Feel like you're paying only for what you use
  • ❌ Costs escalate fast as teams grow (20+ users)
  • ❌ Teams often limit access to control costs
  • ❌ Budget unpredictability when headcount fluctuates
  • ❌ Clients or guests often charged as extra seats

Flat / Fixed Pricing

Rarer, growing

One fixed monthly fee regardless of how many people use the tool. Add users freely — the price stays the same within the plan tier.

Monthly Cost = Fixed Plan Price (unlimited users)

Who uses it:

  • 🟢 ProofHub — $45/mo (Essential), $89/mo (Ultimate)
  • 🟢 Basecamp — $299/mo (flat for all users)
  • 🟢 Teamwork — partial flat tiers for larger plans

Implications for buyers:

  • ✅ Predictable cost — easy to budget
  • ✅ Teams can invite clients, guests freely
  • ✅ No disincentive to collaborate widely
  • ✅ Becomes cheaper per-user as the team scales
  • ❌ Higher upfront cost for very small teams (1–5)
  • ❌ Less common, so requires more education for buyers

Other Pricing Models (Less Common)
03

Freemium / Free Forever

Core features free with limits; upgrades unlock more storage, users, or features. ClickUp and Trello use this to acquire users at scale.

04

Usage-Based

Pay for what you consume — API calls, automations, storage. Rare in PM tools but common in adjacent SaaS (e.g., Zapier).

05

Tiered Features

Price based on feature access, not seat count. Often layered on top of per-user or flat pricing to drive upsells to Business/Enterprise plans.

06

Enterprise (Quote-Based)

No public pricing. Sales negotiates custom contracts with large accounts. Common for Wrike, Smartsheet, and enterprise Microsoft Project.
